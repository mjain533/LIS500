Our Tech Heroes

Kai Cenat (Alex’s Tech Hero)

Kai Cenat is one of the most influential digital creators of this generation, known for breaking records and redefining what success looks like in online media. Starting with viral YouTube videos and comedic content, he later transitioned to Twitch, where he built one of the largest and most loyal audiences in the world. His “Mafiathon” streams—massive live events featuring celebrity guests, creative challenges, and audience interaction—have drawn tens of millions of viewers and generated millions in revenue.

As a Black creator, he has achieved global recognition in a space where representation is often lacking. His rise challenges the implicit biases embedded within digital platforms that can disadvantage creators of color through algorithms and visibility systems. By creating a community-centered brand and reaching audiences across cultural and geographic lines, Cenat has shown that identity and creativity can coexist at a large scale.

Beyond entertainment, Cenat is using his platform to empower others. He launched “Streamer University,” a mentorship program that teaches aspiring creators how to succeed in the streaming industry. His decision to dedicate part of his stream revenue to building a school in Nigeria shows his commitment to community impact. Cenat’s journey illustrates how technology, identity, and power intersect to create meaningful change.

Lanny Smoot (Mayank’s Tech Hero)

Lanny Smoot is a trailblazing engineer and inventor whose work has transformed immersive entertainment. Born in Brooklyn, New York, Smoot earned both his Bachelor's and Master’s degrees in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University. As a Disney Research Fellow and Imagineer, he holds more than 100 patents, making him one of the most prolific inventors in the entertainment industry.

Smoot’s most famous creation is the real-life, retractable lightsaber—a fully functional, illuminated blade that extends and retracts at the push of a button. Beyond this, he helped design Disney’s “Where’s the Fire?” exhibit at EPCOT and developed the revolutionary HoloTile floor, which enables natural, omnidirectional movement in virtual reality environments.

In 2024, Smoot became the first Disney Imagineer inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame. Beyond his technical brilliance, he mentors young innovators from underrepresented backgrounds, emphasizing the importance of creativity, inclusion, and diversity in technology. His career exemplifies how engineering can merge with imagination to shape the future.

Colin Huang (Ethan’s Tech Hero)

Colin Huang (Huang Zheng) is a Chinese entrepreneur best known as the founder of Pinduoduo and Temu, platforms that have reshaped e-commerce in China and around the world. Born in Hangzhou in 1980, he studied computer science at Zhejiang University and later earned his master’s at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. His experiences at Microsoft and Google exposed him to Silicon Valley’s culture of innovation.

Huang launched Pinduoduo in 2015, introducing a “team purchase” model that allowed users to share deals with friends to unlock discounts—merging social engagement with commerce. The platform became one of China’s largest, competing with Alibaba and JD.com. In 2022, his company PDD Holdings expanded globally with Temu, which now operates in more than 90 countries.

Huang’s story highlights how global collaboration, innovation, and inclusion drive success in tech. His cross-cultural experiences reflect intersectionality in action—bridging economic and geographic divides through technology.
